Lemino is a streaming TV service from NTT Docomo that focuses on Japanese dramas, Korean dramas, variety shows, anime, and youth-oriented entertainment. Its catalogue strongly appeals to people in their teens, twenties, and early thirties, especially female viewers and K-culture fans who prefer romance, music-driven content, and modern serial storytelling.

TVer is a streaming TV service that provides free catch-up programming and live simulcasts from Japan's major broadcasters. Its audience spans a broad demographic—from young office workers watching missed dramas on mobile devices to families and older viewers following news and mainstream variety shows—reflecting the general makeup of the Japanese terrestrial TV audience.

Abema is a streaming TV service known for its large lineup of live Internet channels covering anime, dating reality shows, variety programming, news, esports, shogi, mahjong, and cultural events. It is especially popular among teens and people in their twenties and thirties who gravitate toward fast-paced, youth-centered entertainment and always-on channels.

NHK+ is a streaming TV service that mirrors NHK's General and Educational broadcast channels, offering news, documentaries, cultural programs, historical dramas, and educational content. Its viewership typically skews toward adults in their mid-thirties and older, along with families and viewers who value trustworthy reporting, in-depth coverage, and public-service programming.

WOWOW On-Demand is a premium streaming TV service carrying WOWOW's live channels and exclusive films, dramas, concerts, and sports broadcasts. Its subscribers tend to be professionals and higher-income households in the 30–60 age range who prefer high-quality productions, international cinema, and ad-free viewing.
